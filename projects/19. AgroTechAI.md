### 1. Project Title
**AgroTech AI: Smart Crop Doctor & Government Scheme Finder**

### 2. Real-World Context / Business Background
Farmers often face crop losses due to delayed diagnosis of diseases or lack of knowledge about the right treatment. While government bodies publish extensive "Kissan Handbooks" and launch various welfare schemes (like subsidies for fertilizers or equipment), this information is buried in massive PDF documents that are hard to access. Farmers need an easy-to-use, intelligent assistant that can "look" at their crop or "listen" to their problem and provide verified advice instantly.

### 3. Problem Statement
Develop **AgroTech AI**, a digital assistant for farmers. The system allows users to upload images of diseased crops or describe symptoms in natural language (voice/text). Using **Gemini’s Multimodal capabilities**, the system identifies the disease. Then, using **RAG**, it retrieves the specific *scientific treatment* from verified Agricultural Manuals (stored in the vector DB) to avoid unscientific advice. Additionally, the system must parse complex **Government Scheme PDFs** to tell the farmer exactly which benefits they are eligible for.

### 4. Target Users
- **Farmers:** Who need quick disease diagnosis and scheme info.
- **Agri-Consultants (Sahayaks):** Helping farmers in rural centers.
- **Students/Researchers:** Studying plant pathology.

### 5. Core AI Features
The project must include the following 5 AI-driven features:

1.  **Multimodal "Crop Doctor" (Image Analysis)**
    *   *Input:* User uploads a photo of a leaf with spots/discoloration.
    *   *Tech:* Use **Gemini Vision (Multimodal)** to analyze the image and identify the disease (e.g., "Tomato Early Blight") and severity (Low/High).
2.  **Scientific Treatment Retrieval (RAG)**
    *   *Input:* The identified disease name.
    *   *Tech:* The system queries **Pinecone** (which contains chunked text from official Agricultural University Handbooks) to retrieve the exact dosage of fungicide or organic remedy recommended for that specific disease.
3.  **Govt Scheme Eligibility Matcher**
    *   *Input:* Farmer Profile (e.g., "Small Farmer, < 2 acres, Gujarat").
    *   *Tech:* The AI scans the embedded **Government Subsidy Documents** to find schemes matching that profile.
    *   *Output:* "You are eligible for the PM-Kisan Samman Nidhi. Here is the application link."
4.  **Voice-to-Text Interaction**
    *   Since many farmers prefer speaking over typing, allow users to record their query (e.g., *"Why are my cotton leaves curling?"*).
    *   Use an API (browser native or Gemini) to transcribe this to text before running the RAG search.
5.  **Market Trend Summarizer**
    *   Input a specific crop (e.g., "Onion").
    *   The AI generates a brief market analysis based on recent news or data provided in the prompt context (e.g., "Prices are rising due to export ban"). *Note: Use static news snippets for the workshop.*

### 6. High-Level System Architecture

*   **Frontend (React):** A mobile-responsive design with large buttons (for accessibility), an Image Upload/Camera view, and a "Voice Record" button.
*   **Backend (Node.js + Express):** APIs for handling image uploads to Gemini, text processing, and vector search.
*   **Database (MongoDB Atlas):**
    *   Stores: Farmer Profiles, Past Diagnosis History, and Saved Schemes.
*   **Vector Database (Pinecone):**
    *   Stores: Embeddings of **Crop Pathology Manuals** and **Government Scheme Guidelines**.
*   **AI Engine (Google AI Studio - Gemini):**
    *   Used for: Image recognition (Vision Pro model), RAG retrieval, and simplifying complex scheme language into local dialect (English/Hindi).

### 7. AI & RAG Workflow Explanation

**Phase A: Knowledge Base Ingestion**
1.  **Source:** Download a PDF "Handbook of Horticulture" and "Govt Schemes 2025".
2.  **Chunking:** Split the Handbook by "Crop Name" and "Disease". Split Schemes by "Eligibility Criteria".
3.  **Embedding:** Gemini creates vectors for these text chunks.
4.  **Storage:** Pinecone stores vectors with metadata `{ crop: "Tomato", type: "Disease" }`.

**Phase B: The Crop Doctor Flow (Multimodal + RAG)**
1.  **User Action:** Uploads photo of a yellowing leaf.
2.  **Visual Analysis:** Node.js sends image to Gemini Vision.
3.  **Result:** Gemini responds: *"This looks like Nitrogen Deficiency."*
4.  **Retrieval (RAG):** System takes "Nitrogen Deficiency" and queries Pinecone.
5.  **Augmentation:**
    *   *Context:* (Retrieved text from Handbook: "For Nitrogen deficiency, apply Urea N46...").
    *   *Task:* "Explain the treatment in simple steps for a farmer."
6.  **Generation:** Gemini outputs: *"Your plant lacks Nitrogen. Mix 5g of Urea per liter of water and spray it."*

### 8. Expected Learning Outcomes
*   **Multimodal AI:** Handling Images + Text in a single workflow (Vision Models).
*   **Accessibility Design:** Integrating Voice-to-Text for non-tech-savvy users.
*   **Complex RAG:** Filtering vector searches (e.g., searching for "Subsidies" vs "Diseases").
*   **Localization:** Using Prompt Engineering to ask the AI to reply in "Simple English" or translate the advice.

### 9. Constraints & Assumptions
*   **Language:** For the workshop, stick to English for the UI, but you can ask the AI to *output* the advice in Hindi/Regional language if you want to show off.
*   **Data:** Use a small dataset (e.g., just 3 crops: Tomato, Potato, Cotton) to keep the database manageable.
*   **Schemes:** Pick 5 popular schemes (PM-Kisan, Fasal Bima Yojana, etc.) to seed the vector DB.

### 10. Optional Extensions (For Advanced Students)
*   **Weather Alert:** Integrate a free Weather API. If rain is forecast, the AI advises: *"Don't spray fertilizer today, rain is expected."*
*   **Community Forum:** A space where farmers can post pictures and other farmers can comment, with AI auto-moderating bad advice.
*   **Yield Calculator:** Farmer enters "2 Acres" + "Wheat". AI estimates potential harvest based on standard yield data in MongoDB.
