### 1. Project Title
**JurisGenie: AI Legal Research & Brief Assistant**

### 2. Real-World Context / Business Background
Junior lawyers and paralegals spend approximately 30-40% of their time conducting legal research. This involves reading through hundreds of pages of past court judgments to find relevant "precedents" (past cases that help win a current argument). Traditional keyword search (Ctrl+F) is inefficient because legal concepts can be phrased differently (e.g., "medical negligence" vs. "duty of care breach"). Missing a key precedent can cost a client their case. Law firms need a tool that "reads" these documents and extracts insights instantly.

### 3. Problem Statement
Develop **JurisGenie**, a legal research dashboard. The system allows lawyers to upload a library of past court judgments (PDFs/Text). The core requirement is **Semantic Search**, allowing the user to find cases based on *facts* or *concepts* rather than just case numbers. Furthermore, the system must use **RAG** to generate a **Structured Case Brief**—a standardized document summarizing the Facts, Issues, Ruling, and Reasoning—grounded strictly in the retrieved judgment text to prevent "AI hallucinations" (inventing fake laws).

### 4. Target Users
- **Junior Lawyers/Paralegals:** Who do the heavy lifting of research.
- **Law Students:** Studying case law and needing quick summaries.
- **Legal Researchers:** Analyzing trends in specific types of rulings.

### 5. Core AI Features
The project must include the following 5 AI-driven features:

1.  **Semantic Precedent Search**
    *   Users can search using natural language descriptions of a situation (e.g., *"Cases involving compensation for road accidents caused by potholes"*).
    *   The system uses **Pinecone** to find judgments where the *facts* align with the query, even if specific keywords are missing.
2.  **Automated Case Brief Generator**
    *   When a user selects a specific judgment, the AI generates a professional "Case Brief" in a strict format: **Facts of the Case**, **Issues Raised**, **Final Verdict**, and **Ratio Decidendi** (Reasoning).
3.  **"Citation & Act" Extractor**
    *   Analyze the text to extract specific laws, acts, or sections mentioned (e.g., *"Section 302 of IPC"* or *"Article 14 of Constitution"*). Present these as clickable tags or a list alongside the document.
4.  **Opposing Argument Simulator**
    *   Allow the user to input a legal stance (e.g., *"My client is innocent because..."*). The AI acts as the "Opposing Counsel" and uses the uploaded legal database to generate counter-arguments based on past rulings.
5.  **Interactive "Chat with Case" (Q&A)**
    *   A chat interface where the user can ask specific questions about a 100-page document, such as *"What was the dissenting judge's opinion?"* or *"Did the court grant bail?"*. The answer must cite the specific paragraph number.

### 6. High-Level System Architecture

*   **Frontend (React):** A dashboard with a "Law Library" (file manager), a split-screen Document Viewer (Text vs. AI Brief), and a Chatbot panel.
*   **Backend (Node.js + Express):** Handles PDF upload, text extraction, and chunking logic (crucial for long legal docs).
*   **Database (MongoDB Atlas):**
    *   Stores: Case Metadata (Case Name, Year, Court), User Notes, and the Generated Briefs (JSON).
*   **Vector Database (Pinecone):**
    *   Stores: Embeddings of **Case Text Chunks**. Since legal documents are long, they must be split into smaller vectors (e.g., 500 words each).
*   **AI Engine (Google AI Studio - Gemini):**
    *   Used for: Summarization, Argument generation, and Entity extraction (Acts/Sections).

### 7. AI & RAG Workflow Explanation

**Phase A: Ingestion (Handling Long Docs)**
1.  **Upload:** User uploads a 50-page Supreme Court Judgment (PDF).
2.  **Processing:** Node.js extracts text and splits it into **chunks** (e.g., per paragraph or every 1000 tokens). Overlap is important here so context isn't lost.
3.  **Embedding:** Each chunk is embedded via Gemini.
4.  **Storage:** Pinecone stores vectors with metadata: `{ "caseId": "101", "chunkIndex": 5 }`.

**Phase B: Structured Brief Generation (The RAG Flow)**
1.  **User Action:** User clicks "Generate Brief" for Case #101.
2.  **Retrieval:** The system retrieves the most relevant chunks from Case #101 (likely the beginning for Facts and the end for Verdict). *Note: For a full summary, sophisticated prompting or map-reduce summary strategies might be needed, but for the workshop, retrieving top 10 relevant chunks is acceptable.*
3.  **Augmentation:**
    *   *Context:* (Combined text of top retrieved chunks).
    *   *Task:* "Extract the following fields into JSON: { facts, issues, verdict, reasoning }."
4.  **Generation:** Gemini returns the JSON.
5.  **Output:** React renders a clean, styled "Case Brief Card."

### 8. Expected Learning Outcomes
*   **Text Chunking Strategies:** Learning how to handle documents that exceed the AI's context window (a critical real-world skill).
*   **Hallucination Control:** Prompting the AI to answer *only* from the provided text, which is vital in law.
*   **Entity Extraction:** Identifying specific patterns (Laws, Dates, Names) within unstructured text.
*   **Citation Retrieval:** Learning to make the AI "cite its sources" (e.g., "Found in paragraph 4").

### 9. Constraints & Assumptions
*   **Data Source:** Students can use 10-15 sample judgments from public databases (like Indian Kanoon or generic US Case Law) as plain text or simple PDFs.
*   **Legal Accuracy:** The app is a prototype. It does not need to be 100% legally binding, but the *extraction* should be accurate to the text provided.
*   **Language:** English only.

### 10. Optional Extensions (For Advanced Students)
*   **Similar Case Recommender:** When viewing Case A, automatically show "Case B" and "Case C" from the database that discuss the same laws (using Pinecone similarity).
*   **Jargon Buster Tooltip:** If the user hovers over a Latin legal term (e.g., *Habeas Corpus*), show a small AI-generated definition tooltip.
*   **Timeline Generator:** Extract all dates mentioned in the judgment and plot them on a horizontal timeline UI to show the sequence of events.
