### 1. Project Title
**DormDen: AI-Powered Hostel & PG Finder**

### 2. Real-World Context / Business Background
For students and young professionals moving to a new city, finding a Hostel or Paying Guest (PG) accommodation is a nightmare. Unlike hotels, PGs have strict rules (curfews, guest policies, non-veg restrictions). Listings often hide these details. A student might book a place only to realize later that it has a strict "No Late Night Entry" policy that clashes with their night-shift internship. Users need a transparent way to search for "Rules" and "Vibe" before signing a lease.

### 3. Problem Statement
Develop **DormDen**, an aggregator platform for Hostels and PGs. The system should manage listings with detailed "House Rulebooks" (PDFs/Text). The core feature is **Policy-Aware Semantic Search**, allowing users to filter accommodation based on lifestyle constraints (e.g., *"PGs that allow overnight guests and have no curfew"*). Additionally, the system must use **RAG** to act as a "Warden Bot," answering specific questions about the rules directly from the property's legal agreement or rulebook.

### 4. Target Users
- **Students:** Looking for study-friendly environments near colleges.
- **Working Professionals:** Looking for quiet PGs with no restrictions.
- **Backpackers:** Looking for social/party hostels (Zostel style).

### 5. Core AI Features
The project must include the following 5 AI-driven features:

1.  **Rule-Based Semantic Search**
    *   Users search for specific lifestyle needs: *"A place that allows pets and has a kitchen for self-cooking."*
    *   The system uses **Pinecone** to search through the *structured amenities* AND the *unstructured House Rules text* to find matches that strict filters might miss.
2.  **The "Warden Bot" (Policy RAG)**
    *   Every listing has a "Chat with Rules" button.
    *   User asks: *"What is the policy on bringing friends over?"*
    *   The AI retrieves the specific section from the Hostel’s **PDF Rulebook** (e.g., "Clause 4: Visitors allowed until 8 PM only") and answers accurately.
3.  **Community Vibe Matcher**
    *   The AI analyzes reviews and descriptions to tag the property with a "Vibe Label."
    *   Examples: *"High Energy / Party"* (Good for backpackers), *"Strict / Academic"* (Good for exam prep), *"Chill / Independence"* (Good for professionals). This helps users avoid culture shock.
4.  **Hidden Cost Detector**
    *   Analyze the rental agreement or description text.
    *   The AI highlights potential hidden costs: *"Warning: This PG charges extra for Electricity (per unit) and Wifi is not included in the rent."*
5.  **Roommate Compatibility Scorer (Simple)**
    *   If booking a shared room, the user enters a brief bio (e.g., "Early riser, hates loud music").
    *   The AI compares this against the "Room Vibe" (based on anonymized current resident data or existing roommates' bios) and gives a compatibility score: *"Low Match: Your roommates are listed as 'Night Owls/Gamers'."*

### 6. High-Level System Architecture

*   **Frontend (React):** A listing page with "Vibe Badges," a Map view, and a "Chat with Rules" modal.
*   **Backend (Node.js + Express):** APIs for listing management, rulebook parsing, and AI search.
*   **Database (MongoDB Atlas):**
    *   Stores: Property Metadata, Room Types, Rent Pricing, and User Profiles.
*   **Vector Database (Pinecone):**
    *   Stores: Embeddings of **House Rule Documents**, **Rental Agreements**, and **User Reviews**.
*   **AI Engine (Google AI Studio - Gemini):**
    *   Used for: Policy extraction, Vibe classification, and Cost analysis.

### 7. AI & RAG Workflow Explanation

**Phase A: Property Onboarding**
1.  **Ingestion:** Property Owner uploads a "House Rules & Agreement" PDF.
2.  **Extraction:** Node.js extracts text: "Rent 5000. Electricity 10/unit. Gates close at 10 PM."
3.  **Embedding:** Gemini embeds this policy text.
4.  **Storage:** Pinecone stores vectors with metadata `{ propertyId: "PG_101", type: "Rules" }`.

**Phase B: The "Warden Bot" (RAG Flow)**
1.  **User Question:** *"Can I come back after 11 PM?"* (Context: Viewing PG_101).
2.  **Retrieval:** System queries Pinecone for "curfew time late entry" within `propertyId: PG_101`.
3.  **Augmentation:**
    *   *Context:* (Retrieved Text: "Main gate is locked strictly at 10:00 PM. Late entry requires prior Warden approval.").
    *   *Question:* "Can I come back after 11 PM?"
4.  **Generation:** Gemini answers: *"Strictly speaking, no. The gate closes at 10 PM. You would need prior approval from the Warden for late entry."*

### 8. Expected Learning Outcomes
*   **Policy/Legal Text RAG:** Handling "Rules" which are more rigid than general descriptions.
*   **Negative Filtering:** Using AI to *exclude* results (e.g., "Don't show me strict PGs").
*   **Feature Extraction:** Pulling "Hidden Costs" out of a long paragraph description.
*   **Attribute Tagging:** Using AI to assign "Vibe Tags" (Party vs Study) based on text analysis.

### 9. Constraints & Assumptions
*   **Data:** Create 5-10 mock PGs. For each, write a simple text file "Rules.txt" containing things like Curfew time, Food menu, Visitor policy.
*   **Roommates:** Don't build a full social network. Just use a simple text field for "Current Roommates Bio" in the database to run the comparison.
*   **Location:** Use a hardcoded city (e.g., "Kota" or "Bangalore") to keep the map logic simple.

### 10. Optional Extensions (For Advanced Students)
*   **Menu Planner Analysis:** Upload the PG's Weekly Food Menu image (OCR). The AI analyzes nutritional balance (e.g., "High Carb, Low Protein") or flags allergens.
*   **Commute Calculator:** User enters their Office/College location. The system calculates not just distance, but "Commute Ease" based on text descriptions (e.g., "Walking distance to Metro" boosts the score).
*   **Complaint Auto-Categorizer:** For existing residents, a "Complaint Box" where they type an issue. AI tags it as "Plumbing," "Food," or "Wifi" and assigns priority.
