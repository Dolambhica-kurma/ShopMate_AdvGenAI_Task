### 1. Project Title
**ClaimGuard: AI-Powered Insurance Claim Predictor & Policy Analyzer**

### 2. Real-World Context / Business Background
Insurance policies are notorious for being long, dense, and full of confusing "legalese." Policyholders often file claims that are doomed to be rejected because they didn't understand a specific exclusion clause (e.g., the difference between "Flood damage" and "Water damage"). Conversely, insurance agents spend hours manually cross-referencing accident reports with policy documents. There is a need for a tool that can instantly map a specific real-world incident to the exact rules in a policy document to predict coverage.

### 3. Problem Statement
Develop **ClaimGuard**, a policy analysis dashboard. The application allows users to upload their Insurance Policy (PDF) and then describe a specific incident (e.g., *"I dropped my phone in a swimming pool"*). The system must use **RAG** to search the policy for relevant coverage clauses, exclusions, and deductibles. It should then output a **"Claim Viability Score"** (Likelihood of approval) and generate a checklist of evidence required to support the claim, helping users file smarter, accurate claims.

### 4. Target Users
- **Policyholders:** Checking if an accident is covered before going through the hassle of filing.
- **Insurance Agents:** Quickly verifying coverage for clients.
- **Claims Adjusters:** For initial automated triage of incoming claims.

### 5. Core AI Features
The project must include the following 5 AI-driven features:

1.  **Incident-to-Clause Mapping (Semantic Search)**
    *   User describes an incident: *"My car was hit by a falling tree branch."*
    *   The system queries **Pinecone** to find semantically relevant sections in the uploaded policy (e.g., "Acts of Nature," "Falling Objects," "Comprehensive Coverage").
2.  **Claim Viability Predictor (RAG)**
    *   The AI analyzes the *Incident Description* against the *Retrieved Policy Clauses*.
    *   It outputs a **Prediction:** "High Probability" or "Low Probability" along with a reasoning (e.g., *"Likely Approved: Section 4.2 covers falling objects, provided the car was parked legally"*).
3.  **"Exclusion Hunter"**
    *   A proactive check where the AI specifically searches for "Exclusions" related to the incident.
    *   *Example:* If the user says "I spilled coffee on my laptop," the AI checks if "Liquid Damage" is listed under *Exclusions* and highlights it in red.
4.  **Evidence Checklist Generator**
    *   Based on the type of claim (e.g., Theft vs. Damage), the AI generates a dynamic list of required proofs found in the policy text (e.g., *"You need: 1. Police Report (Section 5a), 2. Photo of broken lock, 3. Original receipt"*).
5.  **Deductible & Payout Estimator**
    *   Extract the "Deductible Amount" (e.g., $500) from the policy text.
    *   Ask the user for the "Estimated Repair Cost."
    *   Calculate and display the **Estimated Final Payout** (Repair Cost - Deductible).

### 6. High-Level System Architecture

*   **Frontend (React):** A clean interface with a File Uploader (Policy), an "Incident Report" form, and a Result Card showing the Score, Reasoning, and Financial math.
*   **Backend (Node.js + Express):** Handles PDF parsing, orchestrates the AI analysis.
*   **Database (MongoDB Atlas):**
    *   Stores: User Profiles, Uploaded Policy Metadata, and Past Claim Queries.
*   **Vector Database (Pinecone):**
    *   Stores: Embeddings of **Policy Document Sections**. This is the "Rule Book" the AI consults.
*   **AI Engine (Google AI Studio - Gemini):**
    *   Used for: Reasoning (Matching incident to policy), Entity Extraction (Deductibles), and Checklist Generation.

### 7. AI & RAG Workflow Explanation

**Phase A: Policy Ingestion**
1.  **Upload:** User uploads a 20-page "Homeowners Insurance Policy."
2.  **Chunking:** Node.js splits the text by "Articles" or "Sections" (Definitions, Coverages, Exclusions).
3.  **Embedding:** Gemini creates vectors for each section.
4.  **Storage:** Pinecone stores vectors with metadata `{ type: "Exclusion", section: "Water Damage" }`.

**Phase B: Claim Analysis (The RAG Flow)**
1.  **User Input:** *"My basement flooded due to heavy rain."*
2.  **Retrieval:** The system searches Pinecone for "flood," "rain," "water damage," and "basement."
3.  **Augmentation:**
    *   *Context:* (Retrieved Text: "We cover burst pipes... We DO NOT cover flood caused by natural disasters/rain").
    *   *Incident:* "Basement flooded due to rain."
    *   *Task:* "Evaluate if this is covered. If rejected, cite the specific exclusion clause."
4.  **Generation:** Gemini determines this is likely **Rejected** based on the "Natural Disaster" exclusion.
5.  **Output:** React UI displays: *"viability: Low. Reason: Policy Section 8 explicitly excludes flood damage caused by rain/natural events."*

### 8. Expected Learning Outcomes
*   **Logic Verification:** Using AI not just to chat, but to verify *Rules vs. Reality*.
*   **Math Extraction:** Pulling monetary values (Deductibles/Limits) from text to perform calculations.
*   **Explainable AI (XAI):** The system must cite *why* it thinks the claim is rejected (referencing Section X).
*   **User Flow Design:** Managing the state between uploading a document and querying it.

### 9. Constraints & Assumptions
*   **Policy Documents:** Students should use 5-10 generic "Sample Insurance Policy" PDFs available online (e.g., Car, Home, Travel) to train/test the system.
*   **Complexity:** Focus on *property* or *auto* insurance (easier logic) rather than *health* insurance (too complex with medical codes).
*   **Liability:** Add a disclaimer: "This is an AI prediction, not a legal guarantee."

### 10. Optional Extensions (For Advanced Students)
*   **Voice Claim:** Allow users to record a voice note describing the accident. Transcribe it and then run the analysis.
*   **Fraud Detection Flag:** If the user's story changes (e.g., they edit the description 3 times), flag it as "Potential Fraud" in the database.
*   **Compare Policies:** Upload two different policies and ask: *"Which one is better for a teenage driver?"*
